Emergency & Safety

Know when to stop. Train smart. Protect your body.

Stop immediately if you feel sharp pain.

Do not “push through” pain that feels sudden, stabbing, or abnormal.

Muscle Tear

What to do: Stop, rest, compress, and keep the area supported.

When to stop exercise: Immediately at the first sign of tearing or popping sensation.

When to call ambulance: Severe swelling, deformity, or inability to move the limb.

Dizziness

What to do: Sit down, breathe slowly, hydrate, and cool off.

When to stop exercise: If dizziness persists beyond a minute or returns repeatedly.

When to call ambulance: Fainting, chest pain, confusion, or severe shortness of breath.

Severe Pain

What to do: Stop training. Avoid loading the area and inform staff.

When to stop exercise: If pain changes your movement or increases rapidly.

When to call ambulance: Suspected fracture, uncontrollable pain, or major trauma.

Breathing Issues

What to do: Stop, sit upright, and focus on slow breaths.

When to stop exercise: Any wheezing, tight chest, or inability to recover normally.

When to call ambulance: Blue lips, chest pressure, or severe respiratory distress.

Emergency Contact

In a critical situation, call your local emergency number immediately.
